The Ember Siege: A History of the Fall of Frostpeak
Written by Archmage Eldrin

Part I: The Origins of the Conflict
The year was 842. The Kingdom of Frostpeak, ruled by the aging King Arven, stood as the bastion of Northern culture and economic influence. Known for its massive vaults of raw sky-iron and the geothermal heat sources beneath the citadel that kept its citizens warm in the dead of winter, Frostpeak was a target of jealousy for neighboring territories.
The Ember Lords of Vulcana, a volcanic nation to the south led by Warlord Ignis, coveted Frostpeak's sky-iron and geothermal control to fuel their war machine. Tensions flared when King Arven refused Warlord Ignis's demand to establish trade outposts within the borders. Warlord Ignis declared war, claiming that the North was hoarding volcanic energy that rightfully belonged to all.

Part II: The March of the Flame Legions
In the spring of 842, Warlord Ignis led the Flame Legions across the Ash Pass. Armed with fire-breathing war beasts and elemental siege engines, they quickly bypassed Frostpeak's border guards. The Ember Lords laid siege to the city in the month of Highsun. The walls of Frostpeak, engineered from heavy granite and reinforced with sky-iron, withstood the initial barrage. However, Warlord Ignis's true plan was to block the geothermal vents that heated the city.

Part III: The Siege and the Fall
For seventy days, the citizens of Frostpeak suffered through freezing temperatures as Warlord Ignis's elementalists disrupted the geothermal vents. The water supplies froze, and crop greenhouses failed. Refusing to surrender, King Arven led a final, desperate cavalry charge out of the East Gate on the 12th day of Winterstar.
King Arven fought Warlord Ignis in single combat at the foot of the pass. Although King Arven managed to wound Warlord Ignis, he was ultimately struck down. King Arven died in 842 on the battlefield. With the King's death, the morale of the Frostpeak defenders collapsed. The city fell shortly after, and Vulcana established control over the Northern territory.

Part IV: Faction Control of Frostpeak Today
In the modern era, the ruins of Frostpeak are controlled by the Cinder Vanguard, a splinter faction of the original Flame Legions. Led by Commander Kaelen, they guard the sky-iron mines. However, the Frostpeak Resistance, a loyalist group of Northern rangers, fights a guerrilla war to reclaim their home and restore the lineage of King Arven.
